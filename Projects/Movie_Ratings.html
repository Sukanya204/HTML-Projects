The Best Movies According to Sukanya

My Top 3 Movies of All Time

Zindagi Na Milegi Dobaara

I personally loved this movie because it taught me how to find happiness, even in bleakest situations. When we say Bollywood, the first thing that comes in our mind is Romance. We die over rubbish and overrated romcoms but when it comes to an actually sensible movie, we rarely notice.. ZNMD is a movie that every youngster needs to watch. Its not just a movie where 3 rich people travel lavish locations and come back..The main theme of the movie is to inspire people to overcome their fear; fear of accepting your inner voice, fear of what people think when u take a decision of your life, fear of in fact living your life on your own terms and to do what your heart says. Through a road trip in Europe, 3 friends rekindle their lost friendship, regain strength , celebrate life and learn that YOU ONLY LIVE ONCE.

Yeh Jawaani Hai Deewani

The film follows the lives of four friends — Kabir (Ranbir Kapoor), Naina (Deepika Padukone), Aditi (Kalki Koechlin), and Avi (Aditya Roy Kapur) — as they navigate the highs and lows of life, love, and friendship. It begins with their college days and spans several years, showcasing their journey from youthful exuberance to maturity. Kabir, also known as Bunny, is a free-spirited, adventure-loving guy who dreams of traveling the world, while Naina is a more reserved and academically focused girl. Their paths cross in unexpected ways, leading to a roller-coaster of emotions and realizations.

Dil Dhadakne Do

The film revolves around the dysfunctional Mehra family, who embark on a luxurious cruise vacation to celebrate their 30th wedding anniversary. As the voyage progresses, hidden conflicts, long-held grudges, and unspoken truths come to the surface. The family dynamics are explored through the perspectives of various members: the parents, Kamal and Neelam Mehra (played by Anil Kapoor and Shefali Shah), their children, Ayesha and Kabir (Priyanka Chopra and Ranveer Singh), and the family's domestic staff. The narrative is interspersed with reflections on societal expectations, personal dreams, and familial obligations.
